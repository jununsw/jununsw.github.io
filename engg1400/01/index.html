The Transportation Problem
Background Color:

Problem statement

A firm produces goods at m supply factoires. The supply produced at factory \(i \in \{1 ... m \} \) is \( S_i \). The demand for the goods is spread out at \( n \) market places. The demand at market place \( j \in \{1 ... m \} \) is \( D_j \). Assume that the cost of shipping from factory \( i \) to the market place \( j \) is \( c_{ij} \)

Mathmatical Formulation

\[ \min \sum_{i=1}^{m} \sum_{j=1}^{n}x_{ij}c_{ij} \]

subject to

\[ \begin{align} & \sum_{j=1}^{m}x_{ij} = S_i \;\; \forall i \in \{ 1...m \} \\\\ & \sum_{i=1}^{m}x_{ij} = D_j \;\; \forall j \in \{ 1...n \} \\\\ & x_{ij} \le 0 \;\; \forall i \in \{ 1...m \} , \forall j \in \{ 1...n \} \end{align} \]
